# Research investigation workflow

## Start with the question

A research record should begin from an actual question or uncertainty, not from a vague placeholder.

State:

- the question
- the objective
- scope and non-goals
- why this investigation matters now

## Preserve the investigation, not just the conclusion

A good research record keeps the path, not only the answer.

Capture:

- hypotheses and confidence
- sources and experiments
- rejected paths
- what changed confidence
- conclusions and recommendations
- open questions that still remain

## Link it downstream

Research becomes more useful when downstream artifacts point back to it.

Link research to:

- initiatives when it shapes strategic outcomes
- specs when it informs intended behavior
- tickets when execution depends on the evidence

## Keep research distinct from execution

Do not turn research into a ticket diary.

If the record mainly describes what was implemented or what remains to code, that belongs in tickets or plans. Research should keep its focus on evidence, understanding, and uncertainty.

## Practical rule

If future execution would repeat the same discovery work without this record, the investigation was not documented well enough.
